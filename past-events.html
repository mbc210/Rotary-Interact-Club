Second pinning ceremony

Saturday, May 16, 2026 Hop Lee, Chinatown
4New members
2ndPinning ceremony
8Members celebrating

Right after the morning's care-bag packing, the club walked over to Hop Lee for a lunch that doubled as our second pinning ceremony. Four new members received the Interact pin and a Certificate of Membership in front of the full club and the visiting Rotary Club of Chinatown.

Keeping the celebration in the neighborhood felt right — a busy morning of service, then lunch with the Rotarians who sponsor us. To our four newest members: welcome. We're glad you're here.

Volunteering

110 care bags for seniors

Saturday, May 16, 2026 Transfiguration Chinese School, 29 Mott St
110Care bags packed
8Members on the line
32Service hours

Eight members spent the morning at Transfiguration Chinese School on Mott Street, working as an assembly line — each person stationed at one item, passing the bag down. By early afternoon we had packed 110 care bags for seniors in the community: socks, notepads, lip balm, moisturizer, keychain flashlights, and a handwritten greeting card in English and Chinese in every single one.

The day finished Phase I of the care-bag project. Phase II adds more items based on what community partners tell us seniors actually need, and Phase III is the hand-off — getting every bag to the neighbors they're meant for. We'll report back when they're done.

Cards for our elders

Friday, January 9, 2026 Chinatown

Members gathered for an evening of card-making — laying out printed designs, then hand-lettering each one in English and Chinese: 身体健康, stay in good health, wishing you happiness every day.

The finished cards go to homebound seniors in the neighborhood through our partner organizations — and a fresh batch went into the 110 care bags our members packed this spring.

The Red Run

Saturday, September 27, 2025 East River Esplanade

Our crew took over the East River Esplanade for a morning of laps under the Manhattan Bridge — the Red Run, our contribution to Rotary Day of Service, run in matching shirts alongside our advisors.

Friends from AIESEC joined us at the finish, everyone took a turn at the water station, and we closed out with the group photo we're still proud of.

Arts

Repainting Doyers Street

Thursday, September 5, 2024 Doyers Street Plaza

Every year, Chinatown's most storied street gets a brand-new ground mural — and in 2024 we joined the volunteer crew helping artist Colleen Kong-Savage roll out her waves of blue, red, and gold across Doyers Street.

Suited up in safety vests with the Chinatown BID, our members spent the day filling in the big color fields while the pros handled the linework. By the end, the whole crooked block read as one flowing river of color.

Halloween on the block

Thursday, October 31, 2024 Chinatown

Candy cauldrons, balloon animals, and a table that never stopped being busy — we handed out treats to neighborhood trick-or-treaters at the Chinatown Halloween block party.

Costumes were mandatory-ish (one Magikarp hat did a lot of work), and the candy-corn guessing jar got surprisingly competitive. See you next October.
